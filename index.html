Richard Branson scraps sale of stake in Virgin Atlantic to Air France-KLM

James Dean, US Business Editor

December 3 2019, 12:01am, The Times

  • Aviation
  • Transport

Virgin Atlantic is set to remain in British hands after Sir Richard Branson abandoned plans to sell a significant stake in the airline to Air France-KLM.

The billionaire businessman said yesterday that he intended to retain his 51 per cent stake instead of selling 31 per cent of the airline — set up 35 years ago as a rival to British Airways — to the Franco-Dutch group under a £220 million deal negotiated two years ago.

Sir Richard, the founder of Virgin Group, said in 2017 that he would cede control of Virgin Atlantic by offloading part of his holding to Air France-KLM, leaving him with a 20 per cent stake.
